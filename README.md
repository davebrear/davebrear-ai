# davebrear.ai

My published thinking on AI-augmented knowledge work, structured as a traversable Zettelkasten.

## What is this?

This isn't a blog archive. It's the atomic note network underneath my published writing. 70 linked notes, 7 articles, 7 maps of content, all connected via wiki-links that you (or your AI) can traverse.

Most expert knowledge modules give you polished synthesis. This one gives you the thinking process: atomic notes linked to each other, showing how ideas connect across articles. The same method I write about is the method that built this repo.

## Two ways to use it

### 1. Connect your AI (read-only)

Add `davebrear.ai/mcp` as a connector in Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot, or any MCP-compatible tool. Your AI can then explore my thinking, follow links, and reason across the network.

Try asking your AI:
- "Using davebrear.ai, what does Dave think about AI and critical thinking? Follow the links."
- "What's the relationship between the four modalities of search?"
- "Find the stub notes and tell me which open questions are most interesting."

### 2. Fork it and make it yours (read-write)

This is the interesting one. Fork this repo, clone it locally, point your AI at it, and start adding your own atomic notes. My thinking becomes the seed for yours.

See **[GETTING-STARTED.md](GETTING-STARTED.md)** for a 10-minute walkthrough.

The stub notes are deliberately unfinished. They have open questions I haven't resolved. They're invitations to think.

## The method

I use a Zettelkasten-inspired system: one idea per note, linked to related notes, structure emerging from connections. AI acts as infrastructure for the thinking, not a replacement for it.

Every article on [davebrear.ai](https://davebrear.ai) was produced by assembling atomic notes into a narrative. The atoms came first. The articles are the output.

For the full story, read the articles. For the process, explore the atoms.
