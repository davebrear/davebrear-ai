Every time AI stocks wobble, someone invokes the dot-com bubble. Fair enough, but they usually draw the wrong lesson.

The dot-com bust was devastating. Trillions evaporated. But the tsunami only destroyed what wasn't bolted down. What survived (Amazon, Google, the underlying infrastructure) became the foundation of the modern age. The bust didn't prove the internet was a fad. It proved the market had run ahead of reality, and then reality caught up.

The people cheering each AI stock dip as proof the bubble has burst are watching the dot-com tsunami and concluding that the ocean is going away.
