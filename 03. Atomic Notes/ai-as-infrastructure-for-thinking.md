The value isn't in the tool. It's in the process around it. Watching someone type into a terminal tells nothing about why the output is good.

AI doesn't replace the thinking process. It accelerates it. Because I'd already built the habit of thinking in [[one-idea-per-note|atomic, linked notes]], the AI had something meaningful to work with. Instead of generating from a void.

The AI never sees a blank page. It sees a web of interconnected thoughts built over days. That's why the output sounds like a person and not like a chatbot. The raw material was always human. The [[the-context-layer|context layer]] is what makes this possible, and the [[compound-interest-on-thinking|compound interest]] is what makes it powerful.
