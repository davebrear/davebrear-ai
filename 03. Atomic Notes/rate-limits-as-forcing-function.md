Rate limits forced me to be intentional about how I use AI. The Pro tier offers a genuine taste of what's possible, with guardrails that force optimisation.

The optimisation rabbit hole that followed (trimming context, building tools, eyeing [[local-model-beats-frontier-without-context|local LLMs]]) wasn't about being cheap. It was about understanding what I was actually paying for. Each constraint pushed me to think harder about what actually needed the LLM versus what could be handled locally. It forced me to solve [[the-context-bottleneck|the context bottleneck]] properly instead of throwing tokens at it.

Perhaps guardrails that force intentionality aren't entirely a bad thing. [[optimising-context-for-ai|Optimising context for AI]] became a skill in its own right.
