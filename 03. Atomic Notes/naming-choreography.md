When introducing two related brand names in the same piece, the prose can stumble if both names land in tight proximity. Said back-to-back, the shared word gets cumbersome.

The fix is choreography across paragraphs. The lowercase verb form lands first - the descriptive use that does not yet ask the reader to register a brand. The capitalised method name takes the naming moment alone. The capitalised category name arrives a paragraph later, once the reader has met the method and the brand has earned its weight.

Three appearances, three different jobs. The repeated word now runs as a rhyme across the paragraph rather than a stumble inside a clause.

The work that goes into this happens before any prose gets generated. It is structural - a decision about where each name lives in the piece. Once the structure is right, the sentences carry it.
