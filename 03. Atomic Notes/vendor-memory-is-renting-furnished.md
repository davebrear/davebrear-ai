Most LLM platforms now offer "memory" features. Convenient. But it's their storage, not mine.

At best, I'm reliant on their good grace to offer an export. At worst, it's locked context. Switch provider, start from zero. All that [[compound-interest-on-thinking|accumulated compound interest]], gone.

Then there's security. My organisational context: client names, strategic thinking, decision patterns. Sitting on someone else's infrastructure.

Vendor memory is renting furnished. Feels easy until it's time to leave. [[the-storage-unit-metaphor|The storage unit metaphor]] captures the alternative: own the stuff, rent the space. [[markdown-as-universal-format|Markdown as a universal format]] and [[the-context-layer|owning the context layer]] are the practical answer. [[model-portability|Model portability]] is what keeps the door open.
