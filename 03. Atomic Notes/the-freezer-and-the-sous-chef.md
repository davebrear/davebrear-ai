The cooking metaphor I use for AI shifts depending on the human posture toward it. Both modes describe the same AI, not two different AIs.

When I treat AI as a vending machine - open the box, microwave the meal, plate it, serve it - it becomes a freezer of pre-cooked positions. The output is [[reheated-thinking|Reheated Thinking]].

When I treat AI as a collaborator working under my direction - a sous-chef in my kitchen, with me holding the menu and the palate - the same AI becomes an active participant in the cooking. The output is [[chain-of-cognition|Chain of Cognition]].

The role is mine to assign. A microwave can be the appliance someone uses to reheat ready meals every night, *and* it can be a precision tool a skilled cook uses for specific moves: melting butter without breaking it, controlled defrosting, steaming vegetables. Same appliance. Different relationship. The metaphor describes my posture toward the tool, not a fixed property of the tool.

The microwave didn't change. The cook did.
