The entire credentialing system is built on the premise that knowledge lives in heads and needs to be verified through testing. But what matters more: that you can recall information under exam conditions, or that you've built a system that makes you genuinely more capable? The [[the-experience-gap|experience gap]] already separates people who have figured this out from people who haven't.

"I have a degree that proves I learned things once" is going to compete with "here's a living system that demonstrates how I think and what I know right now." The [[compound-interest-on-thinking|compound interest]] in a well-maintained thinking system is a different kind of credential entirely.

I don't know how this plays out. But the tension between static credentials and dynamic capability is going to get louder. Related to [[what-happens-when-everyone-has-a-second-brain]].
