Evaluating AI as a search engine replacement and concluding it's mediocre is like trying AltaVista in 1997 and concluding the internet wasn't going anywhere.

Evaluated as a search engine replacement, AI is mediocre. It hallucinates. It's confidently wrong. As a content generator, it produces "AI slop". With that frame, hundreds of billions in infrastructure investment does look insane.

The frame isn't unreasonable. It's incomplete. And it's incomplete because most people are using AI in the shallowest possible way. They're stuck at [[three-levels-of-ai-usage|level one]] and judging the entire technology from there. The [[the-experience-gap|experience gap]] explains why the dismissals feel so confident and so wrong at the same time. The [[the-dotcom-comparison|dot-com comparison]] is the better historical parallel.
