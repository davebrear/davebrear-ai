Same utensils, new kitchen layout. I know I will be opening the wrong drawer for a knife for weeks because the muscle memory doesn't transfer.

The stuff moves easily. It's the invisible arrangement that made everything feel effortless. That's what I have to rebuild.

This is why decoupling context from model matters. The invisible arrangement (how things are organised, indexed, and retrieved) is the real intellectual capital. Owning it means I control how the new model finds the knife. [[the-storage-unit-metaphor|The storage unit metaphor]] and [[the-wardrobe-metaphor|the wardrobe metaphor]] are variations on the same insight: the contents are portable, but the arrangement has to be rebuilt. [[model-portability|Model portability]] solves for the contents; the arrangement is the harder problem.
