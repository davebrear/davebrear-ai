When something breaks - a rendering bug, an unexpected behaviour, a passage that does not work - AI and human bring different diagnostic approaches.

The AI runs systematic checks. Byte representation. Line by line. Each candidate cause checked in turn against a hypothesis. Methodical, exhaustive within its frame. When the cause sits inside that frame, the AI is normally first to find it.

The human runs a holistic scan. Eyes across the whole document. Pattern recognition that does not need to enumerate. The cause is often spotted by being out-of-place, not by being checked. Two characters in the wrong shape, a reference in the wrong section, a gap that is obvious if you can see the whole picture at once.

The two approaches are complementary. They have different failure modes. When the AI's systematic check misses something, it is usually because the cause sits outside its diagnostic axes. When the human misses something, it is usually because the search is too local.
