I treat AI output as input, not truth. The responsibility for determining correctness still sits with me. [[scepticism-of-external-ideas|Scepticism of external ideas]] applied to the tool I use most.

I noticed this principle running through every phase of the [[brainstorming-workflow|writing workflow]]: the separation between AI's contribution and my own isn't bolted on as an afterthought. It's built into the structure of the process. AI gives me something to shape, not something to accept.

The moment I treat an AI output as finished thinking, I've abdicated the part that matters. [[authorship-lives-in-judgement|Authorship lives in the judgement]] I apply, and [[firewall-your-thinking|firewalling my thinking]] is how I keep that judgement sharp.
