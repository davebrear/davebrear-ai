The person using ChatGPT to generate a birthday card and the person using Claude with persistent context as a daily thinking partner are having completely different experiences. They're not even using the same technology in any meaningful sense. And yet the first person feels qualified to declare it a fad, because their experience tells them so. The [[three-levels-of-ai-usage|three levels]] describe different technologies, not different degrees of the same one.

They're not wrong about what they experienced. They're wrong about what's possible. That's a distinction worth being precise about. [[the-atrophy-argument|The atrophy argument]] is a variation: genuine at level one, wrong at levels two and three.

The sceptics are right that a lot of what passes for AI usage today is shallow and overhyped. They're just wrong about what that means. It doesn't mean AI is a bubble. It means most people haven't figured out how to use it yet. That's a very different problem. [[ai-sceptics-and-altavista|The AltaVista framing]] captures this perfectly.
