Two paths for the final prose. Path A: write it myself, use AI for feedback. Path B: feed AI my previous writing and have it draft in my voice, then edit collaboratively.

The thing most people don't expect: the two paths converge. If the upstream thinking is solid (the notes, the connections, the structure), the quality of the final piece depends far less on who typed the first sentence than I'd have guessed.

Either path ends the same way. I read every line and ask: would I say this? Does this sound like me? Could I defend this idea in conversation? If no, rewrite. If yes, it doesn't matter who typed it first.
