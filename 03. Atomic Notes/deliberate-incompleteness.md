I no longer view unfinished ideas as a sign of failure or incompleteness. They've become a deliberate holding state.

The [[zettelkasten-as-thinking-system|Zettelkasten method]] makes this practical. I park a half-formed thought as a note, link it to what it relates to, and walk away. When I come back days or weeks later, the [[links-do-the-work|links hold the context]]. Offline reflection on gaps and contradictions has led to some of my most interesting ideas. This is [[compound-interest-on-thinking|compound interest on thinking]] in its purest form.

The urge to resolve everything immediately is the enemy of deep thinking. Sometimes the shape of the problem is enough.
