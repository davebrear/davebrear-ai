I no longer view unfinished ideas as a sign of failure or incompleteness. They've become a deliberate holding state.

The Zettelkasten method makes this practical. I park a half-formed thought as a note, link it to what it relates to, and walk away. When I come back days or weeks later, the links hold the context. Offline reflection on gaps and contradictions has led to some of my most interesting ideas.

The urge to resolve everything immediately is the enemy of deep thinking. Sometimes the shape of the problem is enough.
