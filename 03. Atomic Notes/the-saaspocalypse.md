While everyone was fixated on Nvidia dipping, something quieter was happening. Salesforce dropped 26% YTD. ServiceNow fell 28%. HubSpot collapsed 39%. Adobe, DocuSign, Atlassian, all cratering. [[ai-sceptics-and-altavista|The AltaVista sceptics]] were watching the wrong thing.

These aren't AI companies. These are the companies that AI is threatening to replace. The market didn't dump them because it had lost faith in AI. It dumped them because AI had become a credible threat to the entire enterprise software stack. [[the-rocket-stages-metaphor|The second stage is firing]].

When the market panics not because AI doesn't work but because it works too well, that's a signal worth sitting with. [[the-dotcom-comparison|The dot-com parallel]] says the bust destroys what isn't bolted down, but the underlying technology survives and wins.
