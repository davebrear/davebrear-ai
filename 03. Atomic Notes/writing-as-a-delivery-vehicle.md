Strip writing back to its function and it is transport. A vehicle for ideas to travel from one mind to another. The vehicle's value sits entirely in its cargo.

What you actually do when you write something worth reading is build the cargo: decide the point, frame the problem, walk the reader through the reasoning with the routes you considered and rejected baked in, end with a conclusion you can defend. That is the cognitive backbone, and it is what makes a piece worth engaging with at all. Agree or disagree - that is how you can tell something has been thought.

[[meaning-shaped-slop|Meaning-shaped slop]] is the failure case: a vehicle that looks full from the outside but carries no cargo of yours. The shape, not the substance. The lesson is uncomfortable: the value of your writing was never in the writing. It was in the thinking the writing was carrying. AI made the vehicle cheap; it did not make the cargo any easier to grow.
