Programmers have long practised "rubber duck debugging": explaining a problem to a rubber duck on the desk, because the act of articulating thinking out loud often reveals the answer.

AI is like that, except now the rubber duck challenges my thinking right back. It asks why I'm sure about that assumption. It points out the contradiction with something I said last week. It suggests an angle I hadn't considered. This is [[ai-as-co-thinker|co-thinking]] at its best.

The value isn't in the duck's answers. It's in the pressure the duck applies to mine. [[from-answers-to-pressure|From answers to pressure]] captures the same shift. And the risk of [[echo-chamber-steering|echo chambers]] is why the duck needs to be steered toward challenge, not confirmation.
