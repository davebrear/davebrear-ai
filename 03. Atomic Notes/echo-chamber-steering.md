I learned to watch for the echo chamber. AI tends to confirm existing ideas in an effort to please. It's sycophantic by default.

I actively steer it toward challenge mode: "What's wrong with this argument? What am I not seeing?" This is [[from-answers-to-pressure|pressure, not answers]]. I push it to find where my reasoning breaks. I reject most of what it offers and redirect until the conversation surfaces something genuinely useful.

Without actively steering against confirmation, AI amplifies my existing biases rather than challenging them. This is closely linked to [[scepticism-of-external-ideas|scepticism of external ideas]] and [[firewall-your-thinking|firewalling my thinking]]. The same discipline applies whether the input comes from an LLM, a colleague, or an article.
