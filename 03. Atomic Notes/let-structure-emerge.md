I stopped trying to organise by category. Structure emerges from the connections, not from a predetermined hierarchy. This turned out to be true for my notes and for my AI system equally.

My first month looked messy. That was fine. If it had looked polished in week one, I'd have been spending time on architecture instead of actually using it. When patterns appeared, that's when structure earned its place. [[start-with-a-real-task|Starting with something real]] showed me where structure was actually needed.

When someone hands you a finished system, you never understand why it's shaped that way, and you can't change it when your needs do. [[organise-by-description-not-location|Organising by description, not location]] is the principle that emerged, and [[links-do-the-work|the links]] turned out to be the real structure all along.
