Co-editing prose with AI runs along two axes that are easy to conflate.

The first axis is response: how the human reacts to an AI proposal. Accept the lean recommendation, pick against the lean, agree to leave it alone, or use imperfect feedback as a centre of gravity. See [[four-co-editing-patterns]].

The second axis sits underneath: who actually does the rewriting once the call is made. Sometimes I give feedback and the AI rewrites. Sometimes I just rewrite the line myself. Sometimes both - the AI gets it closer, then I tweak the rest.

The mix is what makes the prose mine. Path B drafting is not a single move; it is many small moves on both axes. By the time the piece ships, the reader cannot tell which sentences came from which axis combination.
