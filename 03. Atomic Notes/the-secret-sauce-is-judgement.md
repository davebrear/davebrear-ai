title: "The Secret Sauce is Judgement, Not Atoms"

The context in a second brain is the atoms themselves. They are the substrate of the thinking. But the atoms are not what makes the thinking valuable.

The judgement, the taste, the nuance for what story wants telling - that is what the thinker brings to the whole thing. That is the secret sauce. It is not something you can replicate.

Phase 2 of [[chain-of-cognition|Chain of Cognition]] is where this concentrates. The AI compresses what is already in the vault, summing up the thinking done to date and presenting it. Then it works with the thinker to expand the skeleton. But the expansion is guided by taste: which atoms come together, in what order, with what emphasis, to make which argument.

That is where the human in the loop plays the most important part. That is where the human adds almost all the value.
