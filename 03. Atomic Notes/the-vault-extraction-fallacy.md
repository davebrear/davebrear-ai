A question that gets asked: if [[chain-of-cognition|Chain of Cognition]] produces a second-brain vault, what stops an employer hoovering the vault, firing me, and keeping 80% of what I know at 0% of the salary?

The technical answer - secure the vault, use encryption keys, make the individual context layer separately owned - has a part to play. See [[personal-encryption-as-talent-market-signal]].

The bigger answer is that the vault is archive, not author. The atoms are ingredients. The judgement that turns ingredients into a dish - the palate, the nuance, the sense of which argument wants telling in which order - is not in the vault. It is in the thinker.

Without the thinker, the vault is a pantry. Extract it and you get a box of stuff, not a cook.

This is the palate defence: an employer can copy the atoms. They cannot copy the taste that assembles them. More than the sum of the parts lives in the thinker's head, not in the vault's file system.
