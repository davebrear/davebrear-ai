After a year of co-thinking with AI, I'm much more comfortable with unfinished ideas and feel less compelled to resolve ambiguity quickly. [[ai-makes-thinking-harder-not-easier|The increased cognitive demand]] taught me to sit with tension rather than rush past it.

What surprised me was how often I approached ideas as in progress rather than in need of completion. Again and again, moments where I had deliberately paused rather than pushed for resolution, choosing to leave questions open once the shape of the problem had become clear enough.

Unfinished ideas aren't a sign of failure. They've become a [[deliberate-incompleteness|deliberate holding state]], a place where tension is allowed to exist without being flattened into a conclusion. [[compound-interest-on-thinking|Compound interest on thinking]] means those unresolved tensions often pay off later.
