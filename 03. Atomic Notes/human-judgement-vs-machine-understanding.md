Graph to semantic is the most interesting composition in the [[chaining-search-modalities|chaining]] of the [[four-modalities-of-search|four modalities]]. Start at a project note. Traverse [[the-link-graph|the link graph]]: these are the notes I deliberately connected. Now run a [[semantic-search|semantic search]] for the same concepts. Compare the two result sets.

The notes that semantic finds but graph doesn't are potential blind spots: connections I should have made but didn't. The notes that graph has but semantic doesn't are places where my judgement goes beyond what an embedding model can detect. Both agree? High confidence. Neither finds it? It probably doesn't exist.

The delta between human understanding and machine understanding is the highest-signal space to operate in. This is where "second brain" stops being a metaphor and starts being architecture. [[the-ai-surfaces-connections-you-missed|The AI surfaces connections I missed]], and I surface connections the AI can't see.
