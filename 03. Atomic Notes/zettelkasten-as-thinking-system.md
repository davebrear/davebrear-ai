The Zettelkasten method, developed by Niklas Luhmann: [[one-idea-per-note|one idea per note]], linked to related notes. Not organised by category. [[let-structure-emerge|Structure emerges]] from the connections.

Luhmann published 70 books and nearly 400 academic articles. His secret wasn't discipline or genius. It was a simple box of index cards with hand-written reference numbers linking one card to another.

Serendipity is the point. Unexpected connections between ideas surface naturally as the network grows. Over time, the network becomes a thinking partner. A conversation with my past self. [[links-do-the-work|The links do the work]], and [[the-writing-is-a-byproduct|the writing is a byproduct]] of the process. [[compound-interest-on-thinking|Compound interest on thinking]] is the long-term reward.
