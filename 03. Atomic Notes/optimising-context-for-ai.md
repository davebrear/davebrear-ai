In the AI age, YAML equals human indexability, filename-based structure equals organisation for AI. Files don't need to be uploaded and processed just to see what they contain.

This was my first real insight about working with AI on files: the organisation scheme matters differently depending on who's reading. Humans navigate by metadata and visual hierarchy. AI navigates by filenames, keywords, and structure. Design for both.

At 500 notes averaging 2,000 tokens each, that's a million tokens of raw content. Getting the right 5-10 notes in front of the AI without burning the entire context window is the real engineering problem.
