[[chain-of-cognition|Chain of Cognition]] draws on layered context, not a single undifferentiated pool of atoms. The layers roughly stack:

- **Employer context**: what does it mean to work at this company? Shared knowledge, culture, policies, institutional memory. Shared across everyone who works there.
- **Role context**: what does it mean to be this particular role at this particular company? Specific remit, responsibilities, relationships. Shared within a role.
- **Individual context**: my thoughts as the person doing this role at this company. The top of the stack. This is where taste, experience, and judgement concentrate.

All of those layers come together to help me work in a chain of cognition. Each layer feeds the thinking. Each layer has a different owner.

The implication for vault architecture: shared context layers can be shared infrastructure. The individual context layer is personal IP. It probably needs to be separable, portable, and, where relevant, encrypted. See [[personal-encryption-as-talent-market-signal]].
