The common assumption about working with AI is that the human needs to articulate what they want precisely. The opposite turns out to be true at the editing layer.

When I do not have the destination clearly articulated - when I know what I do not like but cannot say why, when my own articulation is no better than the AI's draft - the feedback I give is imperfect. The AI does not need it to be perfect. It triangulates direction from the imperfect feedback as a centre of gravity, proposing new options that move toward what I was reaching for.

The thinker does not need to name the destination. The thinker just needs to provide enough pressure for the AI to narrow the space.

This is closer to navigation by feel than navigation by map. The map is fuzzy. The boat finds the harbour anyway.
