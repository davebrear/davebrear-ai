Once I own [[the-context-layer|the context layer]], new capabilities open up. Different tasks can be routed to different models based on sensitivity, cost, and complexity. Not one model for everything. The right model for each job. [[model-portability|Model portability]] makes this possible.

Enterprise context needs classification, audit trails, and retention policies. Self-hosting gives granular control. [[vendor-memory-is-renting-furnished|Vendor memory]] offers none of it. This is where [[the-governance-gap|the governance gap]] becomes a design constraint, not just a worry.

The widening gap: the longer I invest in a portable, governed context layer, the wider the gap between my AI capability and someone starting from scratch with a new model's blank memory.
