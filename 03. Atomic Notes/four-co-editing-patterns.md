When editing AI-drafted prose with the AI in the loop, the rhythm has four distinct patterns. Three are predictable.

1. The AI proposes options. I accept the lean recommendation. The happy path.
2. The AI proposes options. I pick against the lean. The thinker exercising taste over the AI's structural instinct.
3. The AI proposes "leave it alone." I agree or disagree.

The fourth pattern surprised me. I do not have the destination clearly articulated. I know what I do not like. I have a rough sense of direction. I give imperfect feedback. The AI uses my poorly-worded direction as a centre of gravity, proposes new options, one lands. See [[imperfect-feedback-as-centre-of-gravity]].

Working judgement looks like a mix of all four patterns running at once. Not a recipe.
