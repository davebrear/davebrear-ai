The standard worry: AI makes us lazy, we outsource our thinking, our cognitive muscles atrophy. And I suspect that's true if AI is used passively, the way people use Google. Ask a question, get an answer, move on.

But the atrophy argument assumes a passive relationship. In my experience, the relationship is anything but. [[ai-makes-thinking-harder-not-easier|Co-thinking with AI requires more cognitive work, not less]]. The question isn't whether AI causes atrophy. It's whether it's being used in a way that does. [[firewall-your-thinking|Firewalling]], [[echo-chamber-steering|steering against echo chambers]], and [[from-answers-to-pressure|demanding pressure instead of answers]] are all habits that build cognitive muscle, not erode it.

The atrophy is real at [[three-levels-of-ai-usage|level one]]. At levels two and three, the opposite happens.
