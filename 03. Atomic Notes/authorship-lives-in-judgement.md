I'm more willing to stand behind what I produce with AI, not because the synthesis itself is flawless, but because authorship, for me, lives in the judgement I apply to it. The confidence comes not from the output, but from the thinking that shaped it.

The test: could I defend this idea in conversation? If yes, it doesn't matter whether I or the AI typed it first. If no, rewrite it. That's the only filter that matters. [[the-two-paths-converge|Both paths]] lead to the same test. And [[ai-outputs-are-inputs|treating AI outputs as inputs]] is what keeps the judgement mine. [[where-does-the-human-end-and-the-ai-begin|Where the human ends and the AI begins]] becomes a question I can answer precisely because of this filter.
