When I link one note to another, I'm making a deliberate assertion: these two ideas are connected. The links are doing the work: each one forces me to articulate a relationship between two thoughts. This is the [[zettelkasten-as-thinking-system|Zettelkasten]] principle in action, the constraint of [[one-idea-per-note]] making each link meaningful.

Over hundreds of notes, these links build into a web of relationships. Starting from a single note, I can traverse outward and load an entire chain of reasoning in one step. A project note links to meeting notes, which link to related ideas, which link to other projects. One starting point, and the full context unfolds.

The chain of thought is visible. I can see how one idea leads to the next, follow the links, and watch an argument form across a network of connected notes. This network is what makes [[the-link-graph|graph traversal]] powerful as a search modality, and it's the raw material for [[the-flywheel|the flywheel]] when AI starts spotting connections I missed.
