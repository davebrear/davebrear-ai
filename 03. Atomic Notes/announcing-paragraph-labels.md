A reliable AI-voice tell is the paragraph-label that announces what the paragraph is doing. *Why this matters.* *The key insight.* *The big claim.* *The takeaway.* The label flags the move before the sentence has had a chance to make it.

Stripping the label and letting the sentence carry the weight is usually the fix. The same insight, no announcing scaffold, often bolded so the sentence stands alone.

The pattern is so prevalent in AI-authored prose that readers who have developed an ear for it can spot pieces with the label structure even when the underlying content is fine. Drop the label early - at the bullet level, in Phase 2 - so it does not survive into the draft.

Catching announcing labels is a Phase 2 move. By Phase 3 they have already shaped the prose around them.
