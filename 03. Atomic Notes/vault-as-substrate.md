When I sit down to write a piece, the vault is a substrate, not a starting point. The first move is to ask the AI what is already there: surface adjacent atoms, related notes, prior pieces tagged for the topic.

The result is rarely "nothing." More often it is "you have already half-written this." A piece I forgot. An atom that articulates a step I am about to articulate again. A workflow described months ago in different language than I now use.

When the substrate is rich, the work is refactoring, not writing. The plan I tabbed out as a fresh outline becomes a gap analysis - what does the existing material not yet have that this piece needs? Filling those gaps is how the new piece gets made.

Writing without checking the substrate first is reinventing what is already there. Reheating my own cooking - to use the [[reheated-thinking|frame from Cook, Don't Reheat]] - is fine. Reheating someone else's is not. Both depend on knowing what is already cooked.
