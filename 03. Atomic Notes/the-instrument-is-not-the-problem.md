A violin played without skill is grating. That doesn't mean violins all sound like screeching cats. In skilled hands the same instrument makes beautiful music. The instrument isn't the problem. The hands playing it are.

I lean on this distinction whenever [[ai-slop|AI slop]] gets used as a synonym for any AI-assisted output. It isn't. The thing worth attacking is not the tool, it is what some people do with it: the [[reheated-thinking|Reheated Thinking]] loop. Conflating the two confuses the diagnosis and surrenders the instrument to its worst users.

The same instrument that produces grating noise in unskilled hands produces music in skilled ones. Music elevated by the violin but still wholly human. That is the relationship I am working toward with AI under [[augmented-cognition|Augmented Cognition]]: the tool lifts what the human is doing without ever becoming the thing doing it.
