There's a gap forming between what individuals can do with AI and what enterprises can govern. I can build a [[the-context-layer|context layer]] that makes me dramatically more effective. But my employer has no visibility into what's in it, no policy for it, and no tooling to manage it.

[[shadow-ai-follows-the-shadow-it-pattern|Shadow AI is the new shadow IT]]. But the stakes are higher because the context isn't just a rogue SaaS subscription. It's accumulated organisational knowledge, decision patterns, client information, strategic thinking.

Who owns the context when I leave? What are the DLP implications? How do you classify something that's individually harmless but collectively sensitive?

These questions need answers before the adoption curve forces them.
