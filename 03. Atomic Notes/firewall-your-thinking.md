I physically firewall my own thoughts and ideas from external inputs, including AI-generated outputs. They live in a separate area, where I consider them before forming and articulating my own opinions.

This isn't paranoia. It's hygiene. When AI output looks polished and credible, the temptation is to absorb it uncritically. The firewall forces a deliberate moment of evaluation: is this right? Do I agree? Would I defend this?

In the [[brainstorming-workflow|writing workflow]], this shows up as "think alone first" before involving AI. The raw material should always be human.
