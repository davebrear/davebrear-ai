I physically firewall my own thoughts and ideas from external inputs, including AI-generated outputs. They live in a separate area, where I consider them before forming and articulating my own opinions. [[think-alone-first|Thinking alone first]] is the structural version of this instinct.

This isn't paranoia. It's hygiene. When AI output looks polished and credible, the temptation is to absorb it uncritically. The firewall forces a deliberate moment of evaluation: is this right? Do I agree? Would I defend this? [[ai-outputs-are-inputs|AI outputs are inputs]], not conclusions. [[scepticism-of-external-ideas|Scepticism of external ideas]] is the same instinct applied more broadly.

In the [[brainstorming-workflow|writing workflow]], this shows up as "think alone first" before involving AI. The raw material should always be human.
