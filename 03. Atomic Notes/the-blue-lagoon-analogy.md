The Blue Lagoon in Iceland. It's stunning. But it's not entirely natural. The water is a byproduct of a geothermal power plant, pumped up from underground. Humans built the infrastructure. The minerals, the heat, the silica: that's all coming from the earth. It's impossible to tell where nature ends and engineering begins. The result is better than either alone.

The thinking is the geothermal source. The AI is [[ai-as-infrastructure-for-thinking|the infrastructure]]. The output is the lagoon.

The heat was always there, underground. The engineering just gave it somewhere to go. That's what the workflow feels like. The thinking was always mine. The AI just gave it infrastructure. [[where-does-the-human-end-and-the-ai-begin|Where the human ends and the AI begins]] is the question this analogy tries to answer. [[authorship-lives-in-judgement|Authorship lives in the judgement]], not in who provided the heat or the plumbing.
