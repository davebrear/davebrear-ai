I didn't plan my way into a working system. I picked something I actually needed to do, prepping a meeting, and worked through it with whatever I had. The AI got things wrong. It missed context I assumed it had. But that wasn't failure, it was the system telling me what to build next. [[get-interviewed-by-the-ai|Getting interviewed by the AI]] was the first step; real work was the second.

Every useful capability grew from one of those moments. [[let-structure-emerge|Structure emerged]] from real friction, not from planning. The [[the-flywheel|flywheel]] started turning because I had real content to work with, not because I had designed a perfect system upfront.
