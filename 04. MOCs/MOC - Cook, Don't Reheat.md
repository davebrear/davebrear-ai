# MOC - Cook, Don't Reheat

## The Named Concepts
- [[reheated-thinking]] - the loop the article attacks: short prompt, accept, ship as your own
- [[meaning-shaped-slop]] - the output the loop produces: shape of an idea, none of the substance
- [[ai-slop]] - the parent category, with my stance on where it gets misused
- [[augmented-cognition]] - the umbrella the article sits under
- [[chain-of-cognition]] - the method the article points toward, expanded in future pieces

## The Frames and Diagnostics
- [[the-instrument-is-not-the-problem]] - the violin frame: the tool isn't the problem, the hands are. Bookends the article.
- [[writing-as-a-delivery-vehicle]] - what writing is for, and why empty cargo fails
- [[the-dinner-party-test]] - the four-question diagnostic for telling cooked from reheated
- [[the-freezer-and-the-sous-chef]] - why the same AI plays two different roles depending on your posture toward it. The strategic answer to "but doesn't your method also use AI?"

## Connecting Threads
- [[ai-outputs-are-inputs]] - the principle that prevents Reheated Thinking. If output is treated as raw material, not finished thinking, the loop breaks.
- [[ai-expands-not-compresses]] - the article's compression-vs-cognition beat lives here. AI is good at compression; thinking is not a compression task.
- [[authorship-lives-in-judgement]] - the dinner party test is fundamentally a judgement test. Could you defend this in conversation?
- [[firewall-your-thinking]] - the structural defence against reheating other people's positions.
