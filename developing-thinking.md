## Where my head is right now

**The Karpathy question.** [[ai-generated-knowledge-vs-ai-augmented-thinking|AI-generated knowledge vs AI-augmented thinking]] is the distinction I keep coming back to. Karpathy's LLM wiki approach produces 400,000 words of reference material without writing a word. My vault produces positions I can defend in conversation. Both valid, fundamentally different outcomes. I'm not sure the industry is making this distinction clearly enough yet.

**The freezer and the sous-chef.** The same AI plays [[the-freezer-and-the-sous-chef|two different roles]] depending on the posture I take toward it. As a freezer of pre-cooked positions, it produces [[reheated-thinking|Reheated Thinking]]. As a sous-chef working under my direction, it produces [[chain-of-cognition|Chain of Cognition]]. The role is mine to assign. The microwave didn't change. The cook did. This is the strategic answer to "but doesn't your method also use AI?" and the difference between a category and a technique.

**Subscribable brains as a new medium.** Publishing my vault as an MCP endpoint felt different from publishing an article. An article gets read once. A subscribable brain gets queried repeatedly, in contexts I can't predict, by AIs I'll never see. The implications for how I write are only starting to land. I'm writing for traversal now, not just for reading.

**Shared vaults might not work. Subscribable brains might be the answer.** [[can-a-second-brain-be-shared]] is an open question I keep circling. The Zettelkasten method is inherently personal. [[one-idea-per-note|One idea per note]] assumes one thinker per note. Merging two vaults flattens two perspectives into one, and that's where it breaks. But subscribable brains solve this by keeping individual perspectives discrete. My vault stays mine. Your vault stays yours. An AI subscribes to both and traverses across them, finding connections neither of us would make alone. You don't merge the thinking. You federate it. I tested this today by subscribing to Brian Madden's MCP alongside my own vault. Two brains, one session, perspectives intact.

**The governance problem is closer than people think.** [[the-governance-gap]] keeps getting validated. Every conversation I have with enterprise customers surfaces the same tension: individuals building powerful context layers that their organisations can't see, govern, or retain. [[shadow-ai-follows-the-shadow-it-pattern|Shadow AI]] is already here. Nobody has a good answer yet.

**AI-powered knowledge work is silently gaining traction.** The timeline is compressed. November 2025: models jumped in capability and became genuinely good at this. The trigger wasn't abstract intelligence gains, it was practical: models that could reason well enough to be [[ai-as-co-thinker|thinking partners]], combined with tools that let them read and write local files. Writable memory turned a chatbot into a collaborator. Q1 2026 saw rapid evolution of methods across a small group of pioneers, independently arriving at the same patterns. I expect significant momentum before the end of 2026. The adopters are still a tiny percentage of knowledge workers, but they're disproportionately influential, and [[the-experience-gap|the output gap]] is becoming harder to ignore. When colleagues start asking "how did you produce that?" the tipping point isn't far behind. This doesn't make me [[ai-makes-thinking-harder-not-easier|10x more productive. It makes me 10x more insightful]]. That distinction matters because insight gains are invisible until they produce something. Nobody's KPI is "quality of thinking." But the results speak for themselves.

**The decade of transformation.** Even if AI capabilities froze today, there's a decade of work to fully exploit what's already here. Most people are still using AI as a search replacement while a handful have figured out [[ai-as-persistent-collaborator|persistent collaboration]]. That's not a technology gap. It's a practice gap. And practice gaps close through imitation, not invention. The sceptics keep watching model releases and asking "is the next one better enough?" Wrong question. The right question is "have you fully exploited what's already here?" I'm five months in and still discovering new capabilities from the same tools.

**The thesis has a name now.** The umbrella is [[augmented-cognition|Augmented Cognition]]. The method within it is [[chain-of-cognition|Chain of Cognition]]. The enemy it is built to defeat is [[reheated-thinking|Reheated Thinking]] - the loop that produces [[meaning-shaped-slop|meaning-shaped slop]]. The first article articulating this thesis ships Thursday 2026-04-16. A piece a week from there.
