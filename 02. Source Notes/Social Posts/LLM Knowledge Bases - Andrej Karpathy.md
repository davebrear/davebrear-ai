> "Something I'm finding very useful recently: using LLMs to build personal knowledge bases for various topics of research interest. In this way, a large fraction of my recent token throughput is going less into manipulating code, and more into manipulating"

Karpathy's approach: index raw sources (articles, papers, images) into a directory, then use an LLM to compile them into a structured wiki of markdown files. One research topic grew to ~100 articles and 400,000 words without writing a word directly.

Different philosophy to mine. He has the LLM do the writing. I use the LLM to augment my own thinking. His wiki is AI-generated reference material. My vault is human thinking with AI as [[ai-as-infrastructure-for-thinking|infrastructure]]. Both valid, different outcomes.
