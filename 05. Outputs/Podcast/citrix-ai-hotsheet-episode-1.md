Brian Madden and I have started a podcast: the Citrix AI Hotsheet. The premise is simple. Most of the AI conversation gets pitched either by the accelerationists or by vendor podcasts, and almost none of it describes how AI is actually landing inside the banks, hospitals, and regulated environments where most knowledge workers actually work. That gap is the show.

Two topics this week.

**AI will use the apps humans already use.** Brian's thesis: enterprise AI won't arrive through top-down rewrites of every system. It'll arrive through AI workers logging in and using the same desktops, browsers, and applications people already use. We walk through why computer-using agents are still slow today (screenshots, frame by frame), the OSWorld benchmark showing AI now exceeds the median human at operating a computer, and recent research on semantic primitives (W3C accessibility, Windows UI Automation) that cuts token consumption by around 80%.

**The rise of context vaults.** My half. Knowledge workers are quietly building context vaults, what most people call second brains, that compound their thinking with AI. For both of us it started as a folder of notes. It's now the most significant shift in how we work day to day. We get into why it's a corporate blind spot, what breaks at scale, and our experiment of connecting two second brains over MCP so each of us can ask the other's AI what they think.
