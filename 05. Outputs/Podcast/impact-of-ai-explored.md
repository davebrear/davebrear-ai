I joined James and Gerjon on Impact of AI Explored for my first long-form interview about the way I actually work: a context vault, AI as a thinking partner, and the chain of cognition that keeps it honest.

We covered the ground I keep coming back to. What an AI second brain actually is, and why it began for me as a note-taking obsession long before AI entered the picture. Why I bounced off every PKM system until Zettelkasten and Obsidian stuck. The moment AI flipped from a disappointing compression tool ("write this email for me") into an expansion tool that deepens thinking. And the part that gets uncomfortable: what happens to ownership and governance when your most valuable context lives in a vault your employer can't see.

The through-line is chain of cognition. I don't much care who typed the final words of a post. I care where the ideas came from, and whether there's real thinking behind them. That is the line between a second brain and AI slop.
